History

Joseon Dynasty

n 1394, the city of Hanyang (modern-day Seoul) was designated as the capital by King Taejo of Joseon, marking the beginning of its pivotal role in Korean history. Throughout the Joseon era, Seoul became a major center of political, economic, and cultural growth. Many palaces and traditional buildings constructed during this period, such as Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung, remain key attractions in Seoul today. These sites are significant for their architectural styles and royal heritage, showcasing the cultural richness of the era.

Modernization and Japanese Occupation

Toward the end of the 19th century, Seoul began its path towards modernization, highlighted by the proclamation of the Korean Empire in 1897, which reinforced its status as a central city. However, during the Japanese occupation from 1910 to 1945, Seoul came under colonial rule, resulting in significant damage to cultural heritage and the replacement of many Korean structures with Japanese-style buildings. This period was marked by darkness not only for Seoul but also for the entire Korean peninsula.

Contemporary Seoul

Following liberation in 1945, Seoul was restored as the capital of South Korea. The city underwent rapid industrialization and urbanization starting in the 1960s. Hosting the 1988 Seoul Olympics played a crucial role in introducing South Korea to the world stage, and since then, Seoul has emerged as a global city. Recently, fueled by advancements in digital technology and the worldwide popularity of K-culture, Seoul has established itself as a leading center of culture and economy on the international stage.

LocalFood

  • 🍚 Seolleongtang (Ox Bone Soup)

    Seolleongtang became a representative dish of Seoul, especially popular after the Korean liberation. With many large events and ceremonies in the city, there were numerous butchers, who later opened Seolleongtang restaurants.


  • 🍘 Nokdujeon (Mung Bean Pancake)

    Around Cheonggyecheon, a densely populated but impoverished area, Nokdujeon became popular as it could be made affordably using mung beans and an iron pan.


  • 🍛 Chueotang (Loach Soup)

    Many Chueotang restaurants became famous near Dongdaemun Stadium, as the area was abundant with loaches.


  • 🥘 Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cake)

    Instantly cooked in a pot with rice cakes, vegetables, and fish cakes, this style of Tteokbokki is well-known.


  • Seoul-style Bulgogi (Marinated Beef)

    Seoul is known as the birthplace of Jeongol-style Bulgogi, including dishes like Bulgogi stew and Bulgogi in earthenware pots.


FunFacts

  1. Digital Metropolis ☀

    Seoul is one of the most technologically advanced cities in the world, known globally for its high-speed internet connectivity and widespread Wi-Fi accessibility. This technological infrastructure has made Seoul a hub for global technological innovation.

  2. Transformation of the Han River ★

    The Han River, a major river crossing Seoul, was once severely impacted by development and pollution, leading to significant ecological damage. However, over recent decades, large-scale restoration projects have transformed it into a center for leisure and cultural activities. Today, the Han River is a popular retreat for citizens, offering a variety of recreational activities.

  3. Paperless Government ♡

    Seoul is one of the cities aiming for a "paperless government," and has been digitalizing governmental processes since the early 2010s. This shift has significantly improved administrative efficiency and reduced the time and cost associated with paperwork.

  4. Namsan Tower ♥

    Namsan Seoul Tower is one of Seoul's iconic landmarks. Built in 1969 as a communication tower, it now serves as a popular tourist attraction where visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the entire city from the top of the tower. At night, the tower is illuminated with vibrant lights, enhancing the beauty of Seoul's nightscape.
